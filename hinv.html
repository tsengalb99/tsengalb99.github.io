While at Caltech, I started collecting vintage computers, and started the Caltech Vintage Computing Club. I still collect vintage computers, and below is an inventory (hence the page being called hinv) of my collection. Last updated April 2021.

*NIX workstations

SGI O2 (1996) & Personal Iris 4D/25 (1989) - These machines were used by the Goddard lab at Caltech, presumably not for compute purposes since neither the O2 or Personal Iris were particularly fast when new. The O2 has 128MB of memory and the 4D/25 has 24MB, nothing too crazy.

HP 9000 735/125 (1992) - Also used by the Goddard lab, with 400MB(!) of memory in 1992. Runs HP-UX. I tried running NeXTSTEP on it at one point but since NeXTSTEP uses addresses above 64MB for video RAM (thus not liking 400MB of RAM) that wasn't much fun. 640K 64MB ought to be enough for anyone /s.

IBM RS/6000, SUN SparcStation 4/40, SGI Indy, DEC AlphaStation 4/233 (early 90s) - Things I haven't gotten around to turning on.
